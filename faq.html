How do I get back to the home screen if I am lost on my iPad or iPhone?

Press the Home button — the round button below the screen on older devices — or swipe up from the very bottom of the screen on newer iPads and iPhones. This always takes you safely back to your home screen, no matter what happened.

You can never break anything by pressing the Home button. It is always safe to use.

How can I tell if an email is a scam?

Watch for these warning signs:

  • Urgent language — "Act now!", "Your account will be closed!", "You have won!"
  • Suspicious sender address — For example, apple-support-help@gmail.com instead of @apple.com
  • Requests for your password or banking information — No legitimate company ever asks for this by email
  • Links that look odd — Hover over a link (on a computer) to see where it really goes

When in doubt, delete it. Contact the company directly by phone using a number you find yourself — never use a number from the suspicious email.

Is online banking safe for seniors?

Yes — online banking through your bank's official app is very safe. Download the app from the App Store or Google Play Store (search for your bank's name). Always access your bank by typing their address yourself (for example, rbc.com) — never follow links from emails.

Enable two-factor authentication for extra security, and check your balance regularly to spot anything unusual quickly. Your bank will never ask for your full password by phone or email.

What is the difference between an iPhone and an iPad?

An iPhone is a phone that fits in your pocket. It can make calls, send texts, and connect to the internet. An iPad is a larger tablet — about the size of a small book — that connects to Wi-Fi and is ideal for reading, watching videos, and video calls. It does not make phone calls on its own.

Both run the same apps and work very similarly. Skills you learn on one apply to the other.

How do I download apps safely?

Only download apps from:

  • App Store — for iPhone and iPad (blue icon with white A)
  • Google Play Store — for Android phones and tablets
  • Microsoft Store — for Windows computers

Before downloading, check: Does it have thousands of reviews? Is the developer name recognisable? Was it updated recently? If something feels off, do not download it. Never install apps from websites, pop-up ads, or links sent by email or text.

What should I do if I think I have been scammed?

Do not panic — you are not alone, and help is available.

  1. Stop all contact with the scammer immediately
  2. If money was involved, call your bank right away — they can often stop or reverse transactions
  3. Report to the Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre: 1-888-495-8501
  4. Change any passwords that may have been compromised
  5. Tell a trusted family member or friend

Reporting helps protect others. You will not get in trouble for reporting.

How do I make text larger on my iPhone or iPad?

Go to Settings → Display & Brightness → Text Size and drag the slider to the right. For even larger text, go to Settings → Accessibility → Display & Text Size → Larger Text.

On this website, use the A buttons at the top of the page — tap the larger A to increase text size. Your preference is saved for your next visit.

Where can I get in-person technology help in Ontario?

Several free options are available across Ontario:

  • Your local public library — most offer free tech help sessions and workshops
  • Connected Canadians — free one-on-one help by phone: 1-855-808-0505
  • Cyber-Seniors — free help from trained young volunteers: 1-844-217-3057
  • Senior centres — many host technology classes and support groups

See our Resources page for local contacts in Windsor, London, Kitchener, St. Thomas, and Woodstock.

Do I need antivirus software on my iPhone or iPad?

Generally, no. iPhones and iPads have very strong built-in security and do not need antivirus apps. The most important protections are:

  • Keep your operating system updated: Settings → General → Software Update
  • Only download apps from the official App Store
  • Avoid clicking suspicious links

On Android, built-in Google Play Protect scans apps automatically. On Windows computers, Microsoft Defender (built in) provides good protection and does not require a subscription.

What is two-factor authentication and do I need it?

Two-factor authentication (2FA) is an extra security step. After entering your password, you receive a short code by text message to confirm it is really you — like a second lock on the door.

Yes, you should enable it on your email, banking, and social media accounts. It prevents thieves from accessing your accounts even if they somehow learn your password. Your bank and email provider will have a "Security" section in their settings where you can turn this on.

Is WhatsApp safe to use?

Yes — WhatsApp is a safe and legitimate app used by over two billion people worldwide. Messages are end-to-end encrypted, which means only you and the person you are messaging can read them.

A few things to watch for:

  • Only accept messages from people you know. If a stranger messages you asking for money or personal information, block and delete them.
  • Be cautious with links. Even in WhatsApp, do not click links from people you do not know.
  • Download only from the official App Store or Google Play Store — search for "WhatsApp" and look for the official app by Meta.

WhatsApp is especially useful for free calls and messages to family or friends who live abroad, and for group chats with family.

What is the difference between a private message and a public post on Facebook?

This is one of the most common sources of confusion on Facebook. There are two very different ways to write something:

  • Private message (Messenger): Like a sealed letter — only the person you send it to can read it. Look for your friend's photo at the top and a "Send" button. No globe icon.
  • Public post (Timeline/Feed): Like posting a notice on a bulletin board — all your friends can see it, and sometimes strangers too. Look for the "What's on your mind?" box and a 🌐 globe icon and a "Post" button.

The Globe Rule: Before you tap Send or Post, look for the 🌐 globe icon. If you see it, your message will be public. If there is no globe, you are sending a private message.

If you accidentally post something publicly, tap the three dots on the post, then "Edit audience" or "Delete" to fix it.

Is it rude to text someone before calling them?

Actually, it is considered more polite to send a quick text before calling — especially for people you do not contact every day. A short message like "Hi Dorothy, are you free for a call this morning?" gives them a chance to get ready and means they will not be caught off guard.

You do not need to text first if:

  • It is an emergency
  • You have a scheduled call
  • It is very close family (spouse, children) who expect your calls
  • The person has a landline (no texting available)

This is a modern courtesy — not a rule. Calling is still wonderful. A little heads-up text just makes it even better.
